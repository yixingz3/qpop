\begin{abstract}
% Port from paper/abstract.md.
We present a forward-registered, agent-assisted research framework for thematic investment
hypotheses. Large language models are useful for \emph{sourcing} hypotheses but dangerous as
\emph{decision engines}: they can generate plausible but unsupported claims, amplify narrative
priors, recommend more candidates than a disciplined process should admit, and---when evaluated by
backtest---overfit periods that postdate their training data. Open-ended LLM discovery is wrapped in
deterministic gates and a forward pre-registration protocol, so a hypothesis is admitted to a
forward-evaluation portfolio only after surviving falsifiable, dated-evidence checks and forward
(not backtested) validation. Our central finding is methodological rather than a return claim: a
disciplined, forward-registered agent \emph{rejects most of the candidates it surfaces}, with a low,
pre-committed admission rate, while every admitted position remains auditable from the ledger alone.
\end{abstract}
