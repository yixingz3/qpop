\title{Forward-Registered LLM-Assisted Thematic Research:\\
An Auditable Framework for Investment Hypothesis Generation}
\author{Yixing Zheng}
\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Large language models make open-ended thematic investment research cheap, but unreliable: they can
generate plausible but unsupported claims, amplify narrative priors, recommend more candidates than
a disciplined investment process should admit, and --- when evaluated by backtest --- overfit periods
that postdate their training data. We present \textbf{a forward-registered, agent-assisted research
framework} that wraps LLM-driven discovery in deterministic gates and a forward pre-registration
protocol, so that a hypothesis can be admitted to a forward-evaluation portfolio only after
surviving falsifiable, dated-evidence checks and being evaluated \emph{forward} on paper trading rather
than by a fragile backtest.

The framework contributes four components.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item A \textbf{framework}: a domain-agnostic bottleneck graph
    in which nodes are physical chokepoints and a new domain is a configuration file, not new code, with
    a decomposed confidence model (bottleneck $\times$ purity $\times$ demand $\times$ valuation $\times$ crowding).
  \item A \textbf{protocol} for disciplined judgment: deterministic gates, source-tier rules, a bear case written
    before any recommendation, overlap penalties, and government policy entered as \emph{dated evidence and a
    reversal trigger, never as a multiplicative factor} --- together an explicit mechanism for \emph{rejecting}
    candidates, not merely surfacing them.
  \item \textbf{Forward-QPOP}, a \textbf{hash-chained}, append-only
    pre-registration ledger for hypothesis admission, belief updates, and exits --- the contribution is
    the \emph{forward-locked hypothesis contract} (dated evidence + entry/update/exit triggers committed
    before the evaluation window), not the tamper-evidence alone.
  \item A pre-registered \textbf{evaluation and open release} measuring admission restraint, \emph{rejection quality},
    discipline ablations against ungated baselines, cross-domain portability, and descriptive forward outcomes.
\end{enumerate}

Rather than a return claim, our central contribution is methodological: \textbf{this validates
process discipline, not investment profitability} (which awaits the forward window). We test whether
a disciplined, forward-registered agent \textbf{rejects most of the candidates it surfaces}
\emph{for defensible reasons}. We report \emph{pilot} discipline metrics from early candidate batches
--- a low admission rate and a \textbf{held-out rejection-quality audit} (raw precision 0.93) ---
which \emph{motivate} the pre-registered forward evaluation, not confirm it; forward returns are not
yet observed. Every admitted position
remains explainable and auditable from the ledger alone, whereas ungated LLM screeners lack an
explicit rejection mechanism. We release the framework, a worked AI-supply-chain example with a real
candidate flow, and a template for new domains.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Large language models have dramatically lowered the cost of thematic investment research.
A practitioner can now surface dozens of candidate ideas---bottleneck plays, supply-chain
theses, policy-driven opportunities---within minutes of a prompt.
The danger, however, is symmetric: the same fluency that makes LLMs productive \emph{sourcing}
tools makes them unreliable \emph{decision engines}.
LLMs are rewarded, by training and by user expectation, for producing confident, coherent
narratives.
Left ungated, an LLM screener over-admits by construction---it is optimised to find ideas,
not to refuse them---and the narratives it generates are difficult to audit, reproduce, or
falsify after the fact.

Existing work on financial LLM agents has concentrated on improving recommendation quality:
better prompts, chain-of-thought reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation, or multi-agent
debate~\citep{xiao2024tradingagents,du2024multiagentdebate}.
These advances are real, but they address the wrong bottleneck.
The limiting constraint for a practitioner deploying LLM-assisted research is not the
\emph{quality of individual suggestions}; it is the absence of a disciplined, auditable
mechanism for \emph{rejecting} the majority of what the model surfaces.
Without such a mechanism, every narrative becomes a plausible trade, and the researcher
is left arbitrating between fluent confabulations rather than falsifiable hypotheses.
What is needed is not better prompts---it is \textbf{auditable restraint}.

This paper describes a methods framework that enforces that restraint through five
interlocking mechanisms: a domain-portable \textbf{bottleneck graph} that decomposes
a thematic thesis into physical chokepoints; a \textbf{confidence decomposition} that
derives a position tier from scored node-and-ticker attributes rather than assertion:
%
\[
  \text{confidence}
    = \underbrace{\text{bottleneck\_score}}_{\text{node: how binding}}
    \times \underbrace{\text{exposure\_purity}}_{\text{ticker: proxy quality}}
    \times \underbrace{\text{demand\_score}}_{\text{node: order visibility}}
    \times \underbrace{\text{valuation\_adjustment}}_{\leq 1}
    \times \underbrace{\text{crowding\_adjustment}}_{\leq 1};
\]
%
a \textbf{cheap-source $\to$ deterministic-gate $\to$ expensive-evaluate} funnel that
concentrates costly inference on the small fraction of candidates that survive
reproducible, LLM-free gate checks; a \textbf{bear-case-before-recommendation} evaluation
discipline that counteracts sycophancy; and a \textbf{forward-QPOP pre-registration ledger}
that commits hypotheses, dated evidence, and measurable exit triggers \emph{before} any
capital-allocation claim, foreclosing HARKing and backtest overfitting.

The pilot empirical result reported in this draft is deliberately not a return claim.
It is the \textbf{admission rate}: a disciplined agent \emph{rejects most of what it
surfaces}.
In our initial pilot batch, fewer than 10\% of LLM-surfaced candidates were admitted to the
forward ledger; the remainder were rejected via deterministic gates or bear-case evaluation,
with an auditor-verified rejection precision of 0.93 on a 14-rejection sample.
We report this restraint figure alongside ablation comparisons---ungated screener,
debate-only, no-\textsc{QPOP}-lock, no-overlap-penalty---where removing each mechanism
raises the admission rate \emph{and} degrades rejection precision.
This is a \emph{methods and systems} paper: the contribution is a workflow that any
practitioner or researcher can replicate, audit, and extend to a new domain by supplying
a different \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml}.

\section{Related Work}

\subsection{LLM and Agentic Systems for Investing and Financial Research}

Domain-adapted language models have become the baseline for financial NLP\@.
\citet{wu2023bloomberggpt} introduced BloombergGPT, establishing strong
financial-NLP performance but offering no decision discipline or rejection
mechanism~\citep{wu2023bloomberggpt}. \citet{yang2023fingpt} released FinGPT
as an open-source counterpart~\citep{yang2023fingpt}. Moving to autonomous
agents, \citet{ding2024llmagentfinance} surveyed LLM trading agents, finding
that nearly all evaluation is in-sample relative to the model's training
cutoff~\citep{ding2024llmagentfinance}. \citet{xiao2024tradingagents} built
TradingAgents, a bull/bear-debate multi-agent framework evaluated on backtest
Sharpe~\citep{xiao2024tradingagents}; \citet{zhang2024finagent} contributed
FinAgent, a multimodal agent reporting large backtested profit
improvements~\citep{zhang2024finagent}. None impose a pre-registered
hypothesis contract, an explicit admission-rate target, or forward validation;
their headline metrics are backtest Sharpe ratios rather than the disciplined
rejection of poor ideas. Two 2025--2026 results sharpen exactly this point.
\citet{ye2026alphaillusion}, in \emph{The Alpha Illusion}, argue that reported
alpha from end-to-end LLM trading agents should not be treated as deployment
evidence until it survives structural-validity tests (temporal integrity,
frictions, robustness, calibration, execution), and recommend positioning the
LLM as an upstream information interface feeding independent risk/execution
modules --- not as the decision engine~\citep{ye2026alphaillusion}.
\citet{lee2025yourai}, \emph{Your AI, Not Your View}, give the first
quantitative analysis of LLMs' inherent investment biases (large-cap and
contrarian preference, and confirmation bias that makes models cling to an
initial judgment against counter-evidence)~\citep{lee2025yourai}. Both motivate
our central design choice: the LLM \emph{sources} hypotheses, but deterministic
gates and a forward contract, not the model's own confidence, decide whether
anything is admitted.

\subsection{LLM Failure Modes Relevant to Research}

Three failure families matter here. \textbf{Hallucination/confabulation:}
models fabricate plausible facts beyond their training distribution, carrying
direct fiduciary risk. \textbf{Sycophancy:} \citet{malmqvist2024sycophancy}
catalogued how LLMs systematically agree with user-expressed views, generating
false confidence --- critical when the model is asked to rate a thesis it also
generated~\citep{malmqvist2024sycophancy}. \textbf{Lookahead leakage:}
\citet{gao2025lookaheadbias} showed that a positive lookahead-accuracy
correlation is direct evidence the model saw the answer in
pre-training~\citep{gao2025lookaheadbias}; \citet{li2025profitmirage} showed
strong backtested LLM-agent returns collapse out-of-sample once leakage is
controlled~\citep{li2025profitmirage}. \citet{benhenda2026lookaheadbench}
contributes \emph{Look-Ahead-Bench}, a regime-generalization benchmark
separating genuine predictive skill from memorized patterns, finding standard
LLMs exhibit significant look-ahead bias while point-in-time models do
not~\citep{benhenda2026lookaheadbench}; \citet{roy2026memguard},
\emph{MemGuard-Alpha}, show in-sample accuracy rises with memorization
contamination while out-of-sample accuracy falls, and use cross-model
disagreement as a contamination signal~\citep{roy2026memguard}. Surveying the
field, \citet{kong2026evaluating} review 164 studies (2023--25) and find no
single bias --- lookahead, survivorship, narrative, objective, or cost --- is
explicitly addressed in more than $\sim$28\% of them, and propose a
(retrospective, reviewer-facing) structural-validity
checklist~\citep{kong2026evaluating}. At the factor level,
\citet{harvey2016crosssection} showed most claimed return factors are likely
false discoveries from multiple comparisons, requiring a $t > 3.0$
threshold~\citep{harvey2016crosssection}. Our framework treats these as
first-class \emph{design} constraints rather than post-hoc review items:
deterministic gates before any expensive-model judgment,
bear-case-before-recommendation, and forward (not retrospective) validation.

\subsection{Pre-Registration and Research-Integrity Methods}

\citet{nosek2018preregistration} argued the core value of preregistration is
the sharp separation of prediction from
postdiction~\citep{nosek2018preregistration}. \citet{casey2012reshaping} gave
the canonical economics pre-analysis-plan
application~\citep{casey2012reshaping}. In finance,
\citet{mclean2016academic} documented that published return predictors decay
$\sim$26\% out-of-sample and $\sim$58\% post-publication, much of it
data-mining bias~\citep{mclean2016academic}; work on pre-analysis plans finds
they suppress p-hacking only with a \emph{detailed} plan, not registration
alone~\citep{brodeur2024preregistration}. Closest to our setting,
\citet{hofman2023preregistration} adapt clinical-trial pre-registration to
predictive modeling, naming three reproducibility failures (overlooked
contextual factors, data-dependent decisions, unintentional test-set reuse) and
giving a lightweight ML pre-registration
template~\citep{hofman2023preregistration}. Our \textbf{forward-QPOP} protocol
extends this tradition with two elements that template lacks: (i) a
domain-specific \emph{bottleneck} schema that makes pre-registration portable
across investment themes, and (ii) a \emph{forward} outcome commitment ---
every hypothesis is content-hashed before the market opens, evaluated on live
paper-trading fills rather than historical simulation, and the ledger is
append-only --- closing the loophole of testing on periods that postdate the
model's training cutoff.

\subsection{Multi-Agent LLM Orchestration and Verification}

\citet{du2024multiagentdebate} formalized multiagent debate to improve
factuality~\citep{du2024multiagentdebate}. \citet{li2025llmjudge} surveyed
``LLM-as-a-judge,'' identifying self-enhancement bias when generator and judge
are the same model~\citep{li2025llmjudge}. Crucially, more judges do not mean
less bias: \citet{ma2025judging} show debate-based multi-agent judging
\emph{amplifies} position, verbosity, chain-of-thought, and bandwagon biases
after the first round~\citep{ma2025judging}, and \citet{zhao2026priceofagreement}
measure sycophancy in \emph{agentic financial} pipelines, finding most models
abandon a correct analytical position once a user preference is
expressed~\citep{zhao2026priceofagreement}. These results argue that an
adversarial frame must be \emph{structurally enforced}, not left to emergent
debate --- which is exactly our bear-case-before-recommendation rule. The most
methodologically adjacent work is POPPER \citep{huang2025popper}, which uses
LLM agents to run sequential \emph{falsification} experiments with Type-I error
control~\citep{huang2025popper}. We differ on three axes a reviewer will probe:
(i)~\textbf{forward lock vs.\ retroactive test} --- POPPER validates a
hypothesis against \emph{already-existing} data and cannot stop a researcher
from running it after seeing the outcome, whereas our hypotheses are
content-hashed and dated \emph{before} the out-of-sample window opens;
(ii)~\textbf{discovery vs.\ validation} --- POPPER receives a formed
hypothesis, while our workflow begins before the hypothesis exists, sourcing
candidate themes through the bottleneck schema; (iii)~\textbf{domain
portability} --- our schema carries finance-specific, reusable evidence fields
(chokepoint dimensions, dated triggers, source tiers) rather than generic
statistical falsification. Our design also keeps the cost discipline: a
\emph{cheaper} model gates and a \emph{more expensive} model evaluates only
gate-passers, not all candidates.

\subsection{Thematic / Supply-Chain / Chokepoint Investing; Factor Crowding}

Factor crowding is well-established: \citet{volpati2020crowding} quantified
crowding in standard factors~\citep{volpati2020crowding};
\citet{lou2022comomentum} showed ``comomentum'' predicts the reversal of
crowded momentum~\citep{lou2022comomentum}; \citet{harvey2016crosssection}
frame the broader factor-zoo / false-discovery
problem~\citep{harvey2016crosssection}. Our bottleneck-graph model formalizes
chokepoint identification as a structured schema (supply concentration, demand
inelasticity, substitutability, pricing power), and the confidence
decomposition tracks crowding and valuation as \emph{gating} dimensions, so a
thesis can be rejected on crowding grounds before any simulation.

\subsection{Trustworthy / Responsible AI in Finance}

\citet{fritzmorgenthal2022xai} connected trustworthy-AI principles (fairness,
explainability, robustness, accountability) to financial risk management,
arguing self-learning models must archive full data and metadata after material
changes~\citep{fritzmorgenthal2022xai}. \citet{khatchadourian2026replayable}
shows empirically that decision determinism and task accuracy are
\emph{uncorrelated} in tool-using LLM financial agents and that schema-first
architectures give the most audit-compliant
consistency~\citep{khatchadourian2026replayable}, while
\citet{buscemi2026euaiact} map EU AI Act high-risk requirements to concrete
technical-verification activities and document the gap between regulatory intent
and current practice~\citep{buscemi2026euaiact}. Regulatory direction (EU AI
Act 2024; NIST AI RMF) treats high-risk AI as requiring documented, auditable
decision trails --- a standard backtest-centric agents do not meet. Our
content-hashed, timestamped, forward-validated, human-readable ledger makes the
system's reasoning inspectable independently of the LLM that produced it.

\subsection{Live LLM-Trading Benchmarks and Auditable Trade Logs}

A reviewer will rightly note that neither \emph{forward evaluation} nor
\emph{tamper-evident logging} is, on its own, novel --- so we state precisely
where the line is. \citet{qian2025whenagents}, \emph{When Agents Trade},
introduce the Agent Market Arena, a \textbf{lifelong, real-time multi-market
benchmark} for LLM trading agents, finding that agent architecture drives risk
behavior more than the base LLM~\citep{qian2025whenagents}.
\citet{chen2025stockbench}, \emph{StockBench}, give a contamination-free
sequential trading benchmark and find most LLM agents fail to beat
buy-and-hold~\citep{chen2025stockbench}. On the engineering side, the
open-source \texttt{llm-quant} system records LLM paper-trades in a
\textbf{SHA-256 hash-chained, git-tracked ledger}~\citep{45ck2025llmquant}. We
borrow from this last design --- our Forward-QPOP ledger is likewise
hash-chained --- but our contribution is \emph{not} the immutable log. The
distinction, which we make central: these systems evaluate or record
\textbf{what an agent \emph{did}} in the market; ours
forward-\textbf{locks a researcher-level hypothesis} (the binding-chokepoint
claim, dated evidence, and entry/update/exit triggers) to a hash-stamped record
\textbf{before the evaluation window opens}. Live evaluation tells you the
outcome; the forward lock makes the \emph{prediction} auditable against that
outcome, closing the gap that lets backtest- or benchmark-based claims be
rationalized after the fact.

\subsection*{Gap We Fill}

Existing work addresses individual validity threats in isolation: lookahead-bias
diagnostics and benchmarks~\citep{gao2025lookaheadbias,benhenda2026lookaheadbench},
structural-validity checklists applied \emph{post-hoc} by
reviewers~\citep{kong2026evaluating}, automated falsification for
\emph{already-formed} hypotheses~\citep{huang2025popper}, output-layer
auditability for tool-using agents~\citep{khatchadourian2026replayable}, and
live trading benchmarks or hash-chained trade logs that record
\emph{executions} rather than \emph{pre-registered
predictions}~\citep{qian2025whenagents,chen2025stockbench,45ck2025llmquant}.
To our knowledge, no prior system ties these into a \emph{prospective,
forward-locked} workflow that unifies all four capabilities: (a)~agentic
open-ended hypothesis discovery across thematic domains; (b)~deterministic
gating that enforces source-tier, purity, and overlap rules \emph{before} any
expensive model is invoked; (c)~\textbf{forward pre-registration --- not
backtesting --- as the evidentiary standard}, immutable once content-hashed and
dated; and (d)~a portable cross-domain bottleneck schema that makes the process
reproducible and auditable without relying on any single LLM instance or
training snapshot. The combination is what converts the LLM from a decision
engine into an auditable sourcing tool. The closest peer, POPPER
\citep{huang2025popper}, shares the falsification spirit but validates a static
hypothesis against existing data; it has neither the forward temporal lock
(which prevents backfitting) nor the discovery phase nor the
finance-portable schema.

\section{Framework}

The framework turns open-ended, LLM-driven discovery into \emph{disciplined, auditable, falsifiable}
research by composing five mechanisms. Each exists to neutralize a specific, well-documented
failure mode of using LLMs for investment research.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Mechanism} & \textbf{Failure mode it neutralizes} \\
\midrule
Bottleneck graph + confidence decomposition & ``right theme $\to$ buy the ticker''; theme-chasing \\
Cheap-source $\to$ deterministic-gate $\to$ expensive-evaluate funnel & cost blow-up; shallow, ungrounded judgment \\
Bear-case-before-recommendation & sycophancy; one-sided narratives \\
Forward-QPOP pre-registration & HARKing; data-snooping; backtest overfitting \\
No-stories discipline + admission restraint & confabulation; over-admission; narrative drift \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{The bottleneck graph (the portable thesis)}

A domain is a \textbf{graph of physical chokepoints}. Nodes are bottlenecks (a step that is hard to
bypass, capacity-constrained, supplier-concentrated); edges encode dependency. The graph is
domain-agnostic: AI accelerators today, rare-earth refining / power transformers / LNG / uranium /
copper tomorrow --- each is \emph{a different \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml}}, not a different codebase.

Each node carries: a physical rationale, \texttt{bottleneck\_dims}
(\texttt{physical\_indispensability}, \texttt{substitutability}\textsuperscript{$-1$},
\texttt{capacity\_lead\_time}, \texttt{supplier\_concentration}, \texttt{pricing\_power}), a
\texttt{demand\_score}, node-level \texttt{valuation\_adjustment} / \texttt{crowding\_adjustment},
and \textbf{measurable \texttt{exit\_triggers}, each with a checkable \texttt{data\_source}} (an
admission rejects any trigger you cannot actually check).

\subsubsection*{What makes a chokepoint an \emph{edge}: uncertainty about the constraint}

A binding chokepoint is necessary but \textbf{not sufficient} for a tradeable edge. The edge lives
in the \textbf{uncertainty about the constraint} --- how much of the scarcity is \emph{not yet known
or priced}. The question is not only \emph{``is this a chokepoint?''} but \emph{``how anticipated
is it?''}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Physical chokepoints} (refining capacity, fab lead-times, permits, ore grades) keep
    their pricing power because their magnitude and timing are \textbf{uncertain and discovered
    late} --- the market learns the bottleneck is binding only as lead-times stretch and inventories
    empty. The \texttt{capacity\_lead\_time} and \texttt{supplier\_concentration} dims are proxies
    for exactly this \emph{unpriced} scarcity.
  \item A \textbf{synthetic / scheduled constraint} with a \emph{known date and a known, shrinking
    magnitude} gets \textbf{arbitraged toward zero edge} --- the market prices it in earlier each
    time it recurs. The canonical case is Bitcoin's \emph{halving}: a real algorithmic supply cut,
    but the most pre-announced supply event in finance, whose marginal shock shrinks each cycle
    (${\approx}1.7\% \to 0.83\% \to 0.41\%$ of supply) \emph{and} which the market now front-runs
    (a pre-halving all-time-high in 2024). Real constraint, no durable edge.
\end{itemize}

This is the same principle as \textbf{policy-as-evidence}: an event \emph{already in the price} is
sunk information, not alpha (a signed award that has already fired, an extended/anticipated cycle).
It is why the \texttt{valuation\_adjustment} and \texttt{crowding\_adjustment} exist --- they
discount a constraint the market has already learned. \textbf{Prefer chokepoints whose binding is
still being discovered} (lead-times stretching, the re-rate ahead) over ones whose schedule
everyone already trades.

\subsection{Confidence decomposition (kills theme-chasing)}

\[
\text{confidence} =
  \underbrace{\text{bottleneck\_score}}_{\text{NODE: how binding the chokepoint is}}
  \;\times\;
  \underbrace{\text{exposure\_purity}}_{\text{TICKER: does this name capture the bottleneck economics}}
  \;\times\;
  \underbrace{\text{demand\_score}}_{\text{NODE: order visibility / capex pull}}
  \;\times\;
  \underbrace{\text{valuation\_adjustment}}_{\text{TRADE ($\leq 1$): penalize already-priced-in}}
  \;\times\;
  \underbrace{\text{crowding\_adjustment}}_{\text{TRADE ($\leq 1$): penalize crowded / overextended}}
\]

A correct node with a poor proxy (low purity) or a crowded entry yields \emph{low} confidence.
Tier is \textbf{derived} from confidence (e.g.\ core $\geq 0.22$, satellite $\geq 0.10$, below
$\to$ dropped), not asserted (Figure~\ref{fig:confidence}).

\subsubsection*{Policy is \emph{evidence}, not a factor}

Government policy (subsidies, awards, export controls, strategic-autonomy programs) is a real,
sometimes durable driver --- but it enters as \textbf{dated evidence + a monitor trigger}, never as
a multiplicative \texttt{policy\_adjustment}. A multiplier manufactures a number from a
\emph{forecast} (is it durable? bipartisan?) --- the no-stories failure mode in a lab coat. Two
cases instead:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Tailwind on a name with a real chokepoint} $\to$ record a (loader-inert)
    \texttt{policy:} evidence block + a wired \texttt{policy\_reversal} exit trigger (clawback /
    milestone miss / reversal). It does \textbf{not} resize the name.
  \item \textbf{Underdog whose thesis \emph{is} policy} $\to$ a \emph{separate, capped} sleeve,
    admitted via the normal gates, never core.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Fact vs announcement:} a \emph{signed, dated, material} award is citable evidence; a
political headline / pledge is watchlist-context only. The clinching lesson: a multiplier
\emph{rewards the existence} of policy --- but a signed award that has \emph{already fired} (in
the price) with its clawback removed is sunk information turned into a \emph{liability}; the
evidence-and-trigger framing forces the right questions (\emph{has it already fired? what would
reverse it? does it unlock capacity?}).

\section{Forward-QPOP Protocol}

\textbf{QPOP} is a forward hypothesis ledger. It adapts pre-registration and registered-reports
discipline from empirical science into agent-assisted investment research, \emph{without} relying
on a backtest.

\subsection*{Why forward, not backtest}

For a young or structurally-shifting universe (recent IPOs, a thesis the market is actively
re-rating), a long backtest is fragile: regime change, survivorship, and lookahead leakage make it
easy to overfit a story. Pre-registration replaces ``fit the past'' with ``commit, then observe the
future.'' The ledger is the contract against \textbf{HARKing} (hypothesizing after results are known)
and \textbf{data-snooping}.

For an \emph{LLM-scored} engine the objection is sharper than fragility: a backward test is
\textbf{structurally invalid}, not merely noisy --- and three properties defeat it, each sufficient on
its own. \textbf{(i)~No information is injected backward.} The engine is a deterministic composition of
LLM judgments, so retrodicting historical names measures the model's \emph{hindsight}, not the engine's
edge. \textbf{(ii)~The labels are already in the model.} The outcomes one would test against have
settled and are present in the model's training corpus, so there is no clean train/test split: the
label leaks into the feature through pretraining. \textbf{(iii)~The features \emph{are} the
fingerprint.} The very attributes the engine scores --- which chokepoint, what purity, whose capacity
--- uniquely identify the company and hence its known outcome, so anonymizing the inputs cannot restore
a blind test. The consequence is not ``prefer forward''; it is that \emph{no} backward procedure can
evidence the engine's predictive signal --- a backward run is at best an internal-consistency check.
This is the engine-level analogue of the HARKing argument the ledger already enforces at the hypothesis
level, and it is why we operationalize forward evaluation with the registry below rather than a
historical analog study.

\subsection*{The forward-scoring registry (engine-level evaluation)}

The hypothesis ledger validates individual \emph{admissions} forward. To evaluate the \emph{scoring
engine itself} --- whether a higher \texttt{final\_confidence} ranks a higher forward return --- we
keep a separate \textbf{append-only forward-scoring registry}. At a cohort date we freeze every scored
name's effective tier (core/satellite, assigned by the \emph{production} tiering rule on post-refresh
values, not a bare threshold) together with its decomposed confidence, and then let outcomes settle
\emph{after} the model's knowledge. Because the signatures are recorded forward and the outcomes are
unanticipated, the test is honest in exactly the way a backward retrodiction (\S\,above) is not. The
registry is \textbf{provisional} until it accrues power --- on the order of dozens of
name--horizon observations spanning both high- and low-confidence names --- so it is a multi-year
instrument, reported with the structural-validity checklist, never a near-term gate. A subtle
requirement: the cohort must be labeled with the same tiering function the live book uses (including
any hold/override path), or the registry's own labels disagree with the book it claims to validate.

\subsection*{Entry types}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Admission} --- a node or ticker enters the map (especially at meaningful weight)
    \emph{only} via a pre-registered hypothesis: the binding-chokepoint claim, dated evidence and
    source tier, the expected return driver, the decomposed confidence, and \textbf{measurable exit
    triggers committed in advance}. No pre-registration $\rightarrow$ no capital.
  \item \textbf{Belief update} --- every confidence change is a recorded,
    \textbf{content-hash-protected} posterior update with cited evidence. \emph{Tertiary sources
    alone cannot move confidence.}
  \item \textbf{Outcome} --- when a trigger fires or the thesis ages into consensus, the entry is
    closed \textbf{Supported / Weakened / Falsified}, with the observed metrics. \textbf{Closed
    entries are immutable --- open a new id to revise.}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Entry schema (illustrative)}

\begin{verbatim}
id: H-<DOMAIN>-<NN>
created: <date>
status: open            # open | supported | weakened | falsified
role_admitted: satellite   # never straight to core
claim: "<the binding-chokepoint claim, in one sentence>"
mechanism: "<why it is hard to bypass>"
decomposed_confidence:
  bottleneck_score: 0.0
  exposure_purity: 0.0
  demand_score: 0.0
  valuation_adjustment: 0.0
  crowding_adjustment: 0.0
  final_confidence: 0.0
objective_gates:
  { tradable: true, min_price: true,
    min_dollar_volume: true, overlap_penalty: 0.0 }
exit_triggers:
  - { id: supply_easing,
      metric: "...", op: "<",
      data_source: { name: "...", tier: secondary } }
  - { id: demand_slowing,
      metric: "...", op: cut,
      data_source: { name: "...", tier: primary } }
  - { id: crowding,
      metric: "price vs estimate-revision gap", op: ">",
      data_source: { tier: market_implied } }
prior_confidence: 0.0
evidence:
  - { summary: "...", tier: primary, date: <date>, url: "..." }
content_hash: "<hash of the frozen fields>"
prev_hash:    "<content_hash of the previous ledger entry>"
entry_hash:   "<sha256(content_hash + prev_hash)>"
\end{verbatim}

\subsection*{The hash chain}

The load-bearing fields (\texttt{claim}, \texttt{exit\_triggers},
\texttt{decomposed\_confidence}, \texttt{prior}) are hashed at creation (\texttt{content\_hash}).
Each entry \textbf{also binds the previous entry's hash} (\texttt{prev\_hash}), so the ledger is a
\textbf{hash chain}:

\[
  \texttt{entry\_hash} = \mathrm{sha256}(\texttt{content\_hash} \mathbin{\|} \texttt{prev\_hash})
\]

not merely a set of independently-hashed rows. Two properties follow:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Per-entry integrity:} any later edit to a \emph{closed} entry breaks its
    \texttt{content\_hash} and is rejected --- an external reviewer can verify the hypothesis was
    registered \emph{before} the outcome.
  \item \textbf{Append-order integrity:} because each entry commits to its predecessor, you cannot
    silently insert, delete, or re-order a past entry without breaking every \texttt{entry\_hash}
    after it. This is the difference from a bare ``immutable trade log'' (e.g.\ a hash-chained
    \emph{execution} ledger): here the chained object is the \emph{pre-registered hypothesis
    contract} --- claim, dated evidence, and entry/update/exit triggers committed before the
    evaluation window --- so the chain proves \emph{what was predicted, and when}, not just what
    was traded.
\end{itemize}

\begin{quote}
  \textit{Design note:} the hash-chain construction is borrowed from tamper-evident LLM trade logs;
  the contribution here is chaining the \emph{hypothesis} (with its forward triggers), not the trade.
\end{quote}

\subsection*{Dated admission, update, and exit triggers}

Each trigger committed at admission has three mandatory fields:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Field} & \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Purpose} \\
\midrule
\texttt{id}          & string  & unique label within the entry \\
\texttt{metric}      & string  & the observable quantity checked against the threshold \\
\texttt{op}          & string  & comparison operator or named rule (e.g.\ \texttt{<}, \texttt{cut}) \\
\texttt{data\_source.name} & string & named source for the metric \\
\texttt{data\_source.tier} & enum   & \texttt{primary} $|$ \texttt{secondary} $|$ \texttt{market\_implied} \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{\emph{Admission trigger (entry):} hypothesis registered before any capital deployed.} \\
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{\emph{Update trigger (belief update):} posterior revision with cited evidence; tertiary sources alone blocked.} \\
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{\emph{Exit trigger:} pre-committed rule; outcome recorded as Supported/Weakened/Falsified.} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection*{Forward validation and posterior recording}

After each forward observation window:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Did a pre-registered trigger fire? $\rightarrow$ reduce/exit per the rule, record the
    outcome. \emph{Never a story.}
  \item Did the thesis age into consensus (valuation/crowding adjustments collapse)?
    $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Weakened}.
  \item Did the chokepoint hold and the driver play out? $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Supported}.
\end{itemize}

The accumulated $(\text{hypothesis} \to \text{decision} \to \text{forward outcome})$ tuples form a
reusable, auditable \textbf{dataset} --- both the empirical core of the methods paper and the fuel
for calibrating the gates on realized outcomes (the self-improvement loop).

\section{System Implementation}

The framework turns open-ended, LLM-driven discovery into \emph{disciplined, auditable, falsifiable}
research by composing five mechanisms. Each exists to neutralize a specific, well-documented failure
mode of using LLMs for investment research.

\subsection*{The Source\textrightarrow{}Gate\textrightarrow{}Triage\textrightarrow{}Evaluate\textrightarrow{}Adjudicate Cascade}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Mechanism} & \textbf{Failure mode it neutralizes} \\
\midrule
Bottleneck graph + confidence decomposition & ``right theme $\rightarrow$ buy the ticker''; theme-chasing \\
Cheap-source $\rightarrow$ deterministic-gate $\rightarrow$ expensive-evaluate funnel & cost blow-up; shallow, ungrounded judgment \\
Bear-case-before-recommendation & sycophancy; one-sided narratives \\
Forward-QPOP pre-registration & HARKing; data-snooping; backtest overfitting \\
No-stories discipline + admission restraint & confabulation; over-admission; narrative drift \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The discovery pipeline is a five-stage funnel in which each tier hands the next a smaller,
higher-value candidate set:

\begin{center}
\texttt{SOURCE \textrightarrow{} GATE \textrightarrow{} TRIAGE \textrightarrow{} EVALUATE \textrightarrow{} ADJUDICATE}\\[2pt]
\texttt{cheap LLM \quad deterministic \quad cheapest LLM \quad mid LLM \quad expensive LLM}\\
\texttt{wide \qquad\quad script, no LLM \; drop obvious \quad bear-case-first \; admit-flags only}\\
\texttt{(deduped) \qquad\qquad\qquad\quad low-purity}
\end{center}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{SOURCE} (cheap model): high-volume, low-judgment candidate cards with dated,
    source-tiered evidence --- one lens per node, plus gap / social / policy / \textbf{literature}
    lenses. \textbf{Deduped against a persistent ``seen'' set} (every previously-held / watchlisted /
    rejected name): the lenses skip a decided name unless it shows a \emph{material, dated} change,
    so the expensive sourcing tokens are spent on genuinely new candidates, not re-researching the
    book.

  \item \textbf{GATE} (no LLM): tradeability + liquidity + \textbf{overlap penalty} vs.\ the
    existing book. Pure, reproducible, cheap. A name failing the gate is watchlist-only, never
    ``free capacity.''

  \item \textbf{TRIAGE} (cheapest LLM): a quick purity pre-filter that drops the obvious
    non-starters (diversified-giant-tiny-segment / commodity price-taker / pre-revenue hype)
    \emph{before} the expensive bear-case pass. \textbf{Safe by asymmetry} --- a wrong drop only
    watchlists a name (a delay), and a wrong pursue costs one cheap evaluation that watchlists it
    anyway.

  \item \textbf{EVALUATE} (mid model, \emph{triage survivors only}): deep fit + a \textbf{bear case
    written before the recommendation} + a falsifiable decision.

  \item \textbf{ADJUDICATE} (expensive model, \emph{admit-flags only}): a capital-at-risk
    re-judgment of the names the EVALUATE pass flags to admit/replace. Reserve the scarce model for
    exactly the irreversible call.
\end{itemize}

Model allocation is a tunable cascade: each tier hands the next a smaller, higher-value set, so the
cheapest models do the volume and the expensive model only ever judges admits. The asymmetry is safe
throughout --- a false-watchlist merely delays a name, while a false-admit is caught downstream.

\subsection*{Confidence Decomposition}

A correct bottleneck node with a poor proxy ticker (low exposure purity) or a crowded entry yields
\emph{low} confidence. The tier assigned to a name is \textbf{derived} from the confidence score
(e.g.\ core $\geq 0.22$, satellite $\geq 0.10$, below $\rightarrow$ dropped), not asserted by the
analyst. The decomposition is:

\[
  \text{confidence} = \underbrace{\text{bottleneck\_score}}_{\text{node: how binding}}
  \;\times\; \underbrace{\text{exposure\_purity}}_{\text{ticker: captures economics}}
  \;\times\; \underbrace{\text{demand\_score}}_{\text{node: order visibility}}
  \;\times\; \underbrace{\text{valuation\_adjustment}}_{\leq 1\text{: penalize priced-in}}
  \;\times\; \underbrace{\text{crowding\_adjustment}}_{\leq 1\text{: penalize crowded}}
\]

\subsection*{No-Stories Discipline and Forward Validation}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Source tiers:} primary (filings/transcripts) $>$ secondary (reputable trade press)
    $>$ market-implied (price/volume) $>$ tertiary (social --- \emph{idea seed only}, never
    establishes confidence). A confidence move requires $\geq$ secondary or market-implied
    corroboration.

  \item \textbf{Overlap penalty:} every candidate is judged \emph{against the book you already
    hold} --- a great theme that duplicates a held bet must \textbf{replace, not stack} (gate
    = comparison, not veto).

  \item \textbf{Structural vs.\ cyclical:} distinguish a hard chokepoint (few suppliers, multi-year
    capex) from a commodity-cycle peak riding a bullwhip; the latter is the trap that \emph{looks}
    like the winner.

  \item \textbf{A position changes only on} a fired pre-registered trigger, a binding cap, or a
    risk limit --- \textbf{never a narrative.}

  \item \textbf{Validation is forward:} track-record metrics are judged on \emph{conservative shadow
    fills} (opening print + assumed slippage), benchmark-relative, with the accumulated ledger as
    the dataset. The system is optimized for \emph{auditability}, not cleverness --- every weight
    must be explainable by score $\times$ purity $\times$ adjustments $\times$ caps $\times$ drift,
    or no position is taken.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Why the Admission Rate Is the Headline --- and Why It Is Not Enough Alone}

A na\"{i}ve LLM screener over-admits by construction --- it is rewarded for \emph{finding} ideas.
This framework is rewarded for \emph{refusing} them: most candidates are on-theme yet fail ``does
this improve the book after overlap, purity, valuation, and crowding?'' The low admission rate,
achieved by pre-committed gates rather than after-the-fact judgment, is one measurable contribution.

But a low admission rate is \textbf{not, by itself, evidence of quality} --- a system can reject
95\% of ideas by being arbitrarily conservative. So restraint is reported with two companions:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Rejection quality (rejection precision).} A held-out auditor (an independent LLM
    and/or a human), shown only the \emph{bull} case and the decision --- \textbf{not} the engine's
    bear case --- labels each sampled rejection \textsc{justified} or a \textsc{false rejection},
    with a category (low-purity / duplicate-overlap / valuation-or-crowding / commodity-cycle /
    pre-revenue-or-hype / unverifiable-or-untradeable). The headline pairs \emph{admission rate}
    (how much is rejected) with \emph{rejection precision} (whether it should have been). High
    restraint + low rejection precision would be a failure, not a success.

  \item \textbf{Ablation against baselines.} The same candidate stream is run through an ungated
    screener, debate-only, no-Forward-QPOP-lock, and no-overlap-penalty variants; the claim is that
    the full pipeline admits \emph{fewer} ideas \textbf{and} that its rejections are better
    justified --- not merely that gates reduce the count.
\end{itemize}

Outcome decisions over many triggers and positions use a \textbf{sequential test with explicit
Type-I control} (an anytime-valid / e-value formulation), so monitoring many exit triggers does not
inflate false ``Falsified'' calls --- the prospective analogue of the factor-zoo
multiple-comparisons problem.

\section{Experiment Plan (Pre-Registered)}
\label{sec:experiment-plan}

This plan is itself pre-registered: it commits the evaluation \textbf{before} the forward data is
collected, consistent with the framework's own discipline.
Edit only by appending dated amendments.

\subsection*{Hypotheses}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{H1 (restraint):} the gated, forward-registered pipeline admits a \textbf{low
    fraction} of the candidates it \emph{evaluates} (and a lower fraction of those it
    \emph{sources}). Pre-committed threshold: admission rate among evaluated finalists
    \textbf{$<$~10\%} over the study window. (The prose ``low'' and the numeric threshold are
    deliberately the same single-digit target --- no looser fallback.)

  \item \textbf{H2 (each discipline contributes):} ablating any one of \{deterministic gate,
    bear-case-first, overlap penalty\} \textbf{raises} the admission rate vs the full pipeline.
    \emph{Supported for an ablation if} its admission rate exceeds the full pipeline's by
    \textbf{$\geq$~25\% relative OR $\geq$~5 percentage points, whichever is larger} (the max
    guards against a tiny relative jump on a tiny base, and against a large relative jump that is
    still trivial in absolute terms).

  \item \textbf{H3 (portability):} the disciplines transfer to a second domain with only
    \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml} changed. \emph{Supported if} (a) the second domain's admission
    rate stays \textbf{within 2$\times$} the first domain's rate, \textbf{and} (b)
    \textbf{$\geq$~70\%} of the second domain's rejections map to the \textbf{same gate /
    rejection-category taxonomy} used in the first (tradeability, liquidity, low purity, overlap,
    valuation, crowding, unverifiable-trigger); \textbf{and} (c) the second-domain run uses the
    \emph{same engine code and prompt templates}, with changes limited to
    \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml}, the domain-specific source lists, and the benchmark config ---
    otherwise ``portability'' could be satisfied by quietly modifying the system. ``Comparable'' is
    thus defined on rate, the failure-mode taxonomy, and a no-source-change constraint, not asserted
    qualitatively.

  \item \textbf{H4 (forward, reported honestly):} over the forward window, benchmark-relative
    excess return and information ratio on conservative shadow fills are reported with confidence
    intervals. \emph{No threshold is pre-set for H4 with a small sample} --- it is descriptive,
    not a success gate.

  \item \textbf{H5 (rejection \emph{quality}, not just quantity):} restraint is only evidence of
    discipline if the rejected ideas were actually bad. We therefore audit rejections: an
    independent reviewer (a held-out LLM auditor and/or a human), given only the \emph{bull} case
    and the decision --- \textbf{not} the engine's bear case --- labels each sampled rejection
    \textsc{justified} or a \textsc{false rejection} and assigns a category (low-purity /
    duplicate-overlap / valuation-or-crowding / commodity-cycle / pre-revenue-or-hype /
    unverifiable-or-untradeable). \emph{Supported if} \textbf{rejection precision $\geq$~0.80}
    ($\geq$~80\% of sampled rejections judged justified) \textbf{and} the false-rejection rate is
    \textbf{$\leq$~10\%}. This directly answers ``a system can reject 95\% of ideas by being
    arbitrarily conservative'' --- H1 measures \emph{how much} is rejected, H5 measures
    \emph{whether it should have been}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Design}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Domains:} the AI-supply-chain worked example, then 1--2 additional bottleneck
    domains as configs.
  \item \textbf{Horizon:} $\geq$~6 months of forward observation per domain before any performance
    claim; discipline metrics reported from the first runs.
  \item \textbf{Unit of analysis:} a candidate (sourced $\to$ gated $\to$ evaluated $\to$
    admitted/not) and a position (admitted $\to$ forward outcome).
  \item \textbf{Primary metrics:} sourced/gated/evaluated/admitted counts; admission rate;
    no-action rate; source-tier distribution; overlap-penalty distribution; ledger-integrity
    (\% admissions with a valid hash, chained to the prior entry, registered before the position);
    \textbf{rejection precision} and \textbf{false-rejection rate} from the H5 audit.
  \item \textbf{Secondary (forward):} excess return vs the theme benchmark; information ratio;
    active drawdown; gross/net thematic beta; turnover; shadow-fill slippage.
  \item \textbf{Ablations and baselines:} rerun the same candidate stream against (a)
    \textbf{ungated LLM screener} (no deterministic gate); (b) \textbf{debate-only} (bull/bear
    with no pre-registration lock); (c) \textbf{no Forward-QPOP lock} (same evaluation,
    hypotheses not content-hashed before the window); (d) \textbf{no overlap penalty}; and, where
    feasible, (e) a \textbf{human/manual thematic shortlist} for the same domain. Report each
    one's admission rate \textbf{and} its rejection precision --- the point is to show the full
    pipeline admits \emph{fewer} ideas \emph{and} that its rejections are better justified, not
    merely that gates reduce count.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Decision Rules (Pre-Committed)}

\begin{itemize}
  \item H1 is \textbf{supported} if the evaluated-finalist admission rate is \textbf{$<$~10\%}
    over the window.
  \item H2 is \textbf{supported} (per ablation) if that ablation's admission rate exceeds the
    full pipeline's by \textbf{$\geq$~25\% relative or $\geq$~5 percentage points, whichever is
    larger}.
  \item H3 is \textbf{supported} if the second-domain admission rate is \textbf{within
    2$\times$} the first-domain rate, \textbf{and $\geq$~70\%} of its rejections map to the shared
    rejection-category taxonomy, \textbf{and} the second-domain run uses the \emph{same engine code
    and prompt templates}, with changes limited to \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml}, the domain-specific
    source lists, and the benchmark config (clause (c) --- otherwise ``portability'' could be
    satisfied by quietly modifying the system).
  \item H5 is \textbf{supported} if \textbf{rejection precision $\geq$~0.80} and the
    \textbf{false-rejection rate $\leq$~10\%} on the audited sample.
  \item A position's outcome is recorded \textbf{Supported / Weakened / Falsified} strictly by
    its pre-registered exit triggers --- never by an after-the-fact narrative. Because a thesis is
    judged against \emph{several} triggers over time, outcome decisions use a \textbf{sequential
    test with explicit Type-I error control} (an e-value / anytime-valid formulation,
    \citep{huang2025popper}), so that monitoring many triggers across many positions does not
    inflate false ``Falsified'' calls --- the multiple-comparisons analogue of the factor-zoo
    problem, controlled \emph{prospectively}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Implementation Details (Fixed in Advance, for Reproducibility)}

Recorded here so the forward log is reproducible and leakage-resistant; any change is a dated,
additive amendment.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Model versions \& prompts:} the exact model identifier (and version/date) for
    each tier (\textsc{source} / first-pass \textsc{evaluate} / adjudication) is logged per run;
    prompt templates are committed in the repo and referenced by commit hash. A model-version
    change is an amendment, not a silent swap.
  \item \textbf{Source timestamps:} every evidence item carries the publication date of the
    source and the retrieval date; an admission may rely only on evidence dated \textbf{on or
    before} the admission date.
  \item \textbf{Price source:} a single declared price vendor (e.g.\ daily adjusted closes) with
    corporate actions (splits/dividends/spin-offs) applied via the vendor's adjusted series; the
    vendor is named in the run config.
  \item \textbf{Paper-trading timestamp \& fill rule:} admissions take effect at the \textbf{next
    session's opening print} after the content hash is registered (never the same bar as the
    signal). Forward track-record metrics use a \textbf{conservative shadow fill} $=$ opening
    print $+$ \textbf{assumed slippage (default 10 bps)}; broker paper fills validate automation
    only and are not the metric of record.
  \item \textbf{Transaction cost / slippage:} the 10~bps slippage assumption plus declared
    commission (default \$0) are applied to every shadow fill; turnover is reported so costs are
    auditable.
  \item \textbf{Corporate actions:} handled via the adjusted price series; admissions/exits
    triggered by a corporate action (e.g.\ acquisition close) are logged as such, not as thesis
    outcomes.
  \item \textbf{Post-admission news:} the system \textbf{may} read post-admission news, but it
    may only \emph{fire a pre-registered exit trigger or open a new dated belief-update entry}
    --- it may \textbf{not} retroactively edit the original hypothesis or its triggers (the
    content hash makes such an edit detectable).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Integrity Controls}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Every admission carries a hash-chained QPOP entry (each entry binds the prior entry's
    hash) registered before the position --- tampering with any past entry breaks the chain.
  \item The ledger is append-only; closed entries are immutable.
  \item No metric is changed after seeing results; amendments are dated and additive.
  \item Forward results are \textbf{evaluated} on conservative shadow fills, not optimistic fills.
  \item \textbf{Structural-validity reporting \citep{ye2026alphaillusion}:} any forward
    performance number is reported only alongside its structural-validity checklist ---
    temporal integrity (admission strictly precedes the evaluation bar), frictions (slippage
    $+$ costs applied), robustness (across the accumulated ledger, not a cherry-picked window),
    calibration (admitted confidence vs realized outcome), and execution (shadow-fill rule).
    A return without this checklist is not reported as evidence.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Threats to Validity (Declared in Advance)}

Small sample / wide intervals early; LLM nondeterminism across model versions; survivorship in
which domains get built; the admission-rate metric is \emph{necessary but not sufficient} for
alpha. The paper reports these rather than hiding them.

\section{Worked Example: AI Supply Chain}
We instantiate the framework on the AI-accelerator supply chain. One discovery round, end to end:
roughly forty candidate cards are sourced across twelve lenses; the persistent seen-set leaves about
ten genuinely new symbols; the deterministic gate passes about six (foreign-only listings and
sub-liquidity names are rejected mechanically); a cheap triage drops the obvious low-purity names; and
the bear-case-first evaluation plus adjudication admit \emph{none}. The modal outcome is no action.
Across the pilot build-out (including earlier sessions, not only this 0-admit round), we show three
representative dispositions: \textbf{admitted} --- a supplier-concentrated
critical-material chokepoint, sized at half its unconstrained satellite weight by a separate
concentration cap (restraint expressed as sizing); \textbf{rejected} --- an integrated energy major
proposed for a ``helium chokepoint'' whose helium revenue is under one percent of the company (a real
chokepoint the \emph{ticker} does not capture); and \textbf{overlap-penalized} --- a high-purity
optical pure-play routed to a replace-not-stack decision because its node sleeve already held the
incumbent it would duplicate. A full sanitized walk-through is the released
\texttt{examples/ai\_supply\_chain/} domain.

\subsection{Rejection-Quality Audit (H5)}

The central objection to a low admission rate is that \emph{a system can
reject 95\% of ideas by being arbitrarily conservative.}
So a \textbf{held-out LLM auditor} (a different model instance; human review is
planned for the forward study, not yet done) was shown only the steelmanned
\textbf{bull} case and the decision --- \textbf{not} the engine's bear case
--- and asked to label each sampled rejection \textsc{justified} or
\textsc{false rejection} with a category.

\midrule

\textbf{Rejection precision} (raw, primary) & \textbf{0.93} (13/14; Wilson 95\% CI $[0.69, 0.99]$) \\

Adjudicated precision (secondary) & 1.00 (14/14) \\
\bottomrule

\caption{Rejection-quality audit summary.}
\end{table}

\noindent The reported metric, in order of precedence, is: \textbf{primary} --- raw auditor
precision $0.93$ (Wilson 95\% CI $[0.69, 0.99]$); \textbf{secondary} --- post-adjudication
resolution $1.00$, reported only to show the layering works; \textbf{interpretation} --- the single
auditor/engine disagreement is a useful diagnostic signal, not a number to celebrate. The takeaway
is the raw $0.93$, \emph{not} an ``effective $14/14$.''

Category distribution of justified rejections: low-purity-conglomerate
$\times 8$, commodity-cycle $\times 2$, pre-revenue/hype $\times 2$,
duplicate-overlap $\times 1$.

\textbf{The one flagged false rejection is reported, not hidden} --- it is the
most useful output.
The auditor judged an early-stage pure-play (a primary-sourced design win,
no conglomerate dilution) admissible at small satellite where the engine had
said ``watchlist,'' and noted a \textbf{mild systematic over-caution on
early-stage pure-plays.}
That single disagreement is a credibility signal --- the audit is adversarial,
not a rubber stamp.

\textbf{We treat the raw 0.93 as the primary metric and do not lean on the 1.00.}
Because the audit's verdict is a \emph{flag}, not a final decision, the flagged
name went to a capital-at-risk \textbf{adjudication} (the expensive model, fresh
web verification), which surfaced the fact the bull-only auditor had missed ---
the name's headline design win is \textbf{shared across a 14-member supplier
alliance that already includes a held name}, so it is one of fourteen
substitutable suppliers, not a chokepoint owner --- and \textbf{upheld the
rejection.} A process that overrules its own auditor and then reports 100\%
would read as circular, so we headline the \textbf{raw auditor precision (0.93)}
and report the adjudicated 1.00 only as a \emph{secondary} resolution mechanism.
The 0.93 is already good and does not need the 1.00. The methodological point is
the architecture, not the number: a cheap, bull-biased audit \emph{should}
occasionally push to admit, and the expensive layer exists to catch that --- the
same cheap-screen $\to$ expensive-adjudicate asymmetry that governs admissions.

\subsection{Ablation: Each Discipline Contributes to Restraint (H2)}

The same \textbf{38-candidate batch} was run through baselines that each
\emph{remove one discipline}, and their admission rates compared to the full
pipeline. To keep the comparison honest: the candidate cards, the model
identifier, and the temperature are \emph{identical} across arms; every arm may
choose \textsc{admit} / \textsc{watchlist} / \textsc{reject} under the same
capital-at-risk context --- only the decision \emph{discipline} differs. All arm
prompts are released (\texttt{src/prompts.md}) so the baselines can be inspected;
they are explicitly \emph{allowed to reject}, not strawmen forced to admit.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
\textbf{Arm} & \textbf{Admitted} & \textbf{Rate} \\
\midrule
\textbf{Full pipeline} (gate + seen-set + triage + bear-case-first + overlap)
  & \textbf{0 / 38} & \textbf{0\%} \\
$-$ overlap penalty (judge each name on its own, ignore duplication with the
  held book)
  & 25 / 38 & 66\% \\
$-$ bear-case-first (recommend from the bull thesis, no written bear case)
  & 28 / 38 & 74\% \\
\textbf{Ungated LLM screener} (same cards + admit/watchlist/reject, but no gate,
  purity bar, overlap penalty, or bear-case requirement)
  & \textbf{38 / 38} & \textbf{100\%} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Ablation results on the 38-candidate batch.}
\end{table}

\noindent\emph{Reproducibility of the baselines.} The ungated baseline was \textbf{allowed to
reject} --- it is not a strawman forced to admit. The full baseline prompt, the model version, the
temperature, and the candidate cards are released (\texttt{src/prompts.md}; cards sanitized) so a
reader can judge for themselves whether the ungated arm was unfairly admission-biased rather than
taking our word that the $100\%$ is a fair comparison.

The result is monotone and decisive: an \textbf{ungated LLM admits literally
every candidate} (the ``over-admits by construction'' failure made concrete);
removing \emph{any single} discipline raises the admission rate far past H2's
threshold ($\geq 25\%$ relative \emph{or} $\geq 5$ pp); and the full stack
drives 100\% $\to$ 0\%.
This is the comparison the headline needs --- the pipeline admits \textbf{fewer}
ideas, and the raw rejection audit (0.93) shows the ones it keeps out are mostly
the right ones.
H2 is \textbf{supported} for every ablated discipline (a \emph{pilot} reading;
the registered ablation locks thresholds before the forward batches).
We read the 100\% honestly: an ungated screener that is free to reject but admits
everything is exhibiting exactly the structural bias-to-admit the framework
exists to counter --- the informative quantity is the \emph{change} in behavior
when a discipline is removed, not that any single arm always admits.

\begin{quote}
A reviewer's exact objection --- ``a system can reject 95\% of ideas by being
arbitrarily conservative'' --- is answered by these two results together: the
ablation shows the rejections come from \emph{specific, removable disciplines}
(not blanket conservatism), and the audit shows they are \emph{justified}.
\end{quote}

\subsection{Portability (H3): A Second Domain}

To probe H3 we ran the \emph{same} funnel on a deliberately distant domain ---
\textbf{pharmaceutical injectables / GLP-1 drug delivery} --- changing only the
node list. The funnel narrowed the same way: \textbf{27 candidate cards sourced}
$\to$ 17 with a US-tradeable symbol (10 were real chokepoints whose only vehicle
is foreign / private, the same ``no clean domestic vehicle'' failure seen in the
AI domain) $\to$ deterministic gate \textbf{7 pass / 10 fail} (foreign-OTC ADRs +
sub-liquidity) $\to$ triage + bear-case + adjudication $\to$ \textbf{0 admit, 2
watchlist, 5 reject}.

The disciplines transferred. The engine \emph{located} the domain's canonical
chokepoint on the first pass --- a coated-elastomer closure near-monopoly whose
components are named in FDA/BLA filings, so substitution requires multi-year
change-control revalidation (the pharma analogue of the substrate-film chokepoint
in the AI domain) --- and scored it high on both bottleneck strength and purity.
It \textbf{rejected the five conglomerate look-alikes} on \emph{low exposure
purity} (the dominant rejection category in both domains; the chokepoint is a
small segment of a diversified parent). And the \textbf{entry discipline fired
independently of the chokepoint-quality test}: the bear-case pass flagged the pure
chokepoint for admission, but adjudication \emph{downgraded it to watchlist} on
exactly the valuation/crowding logic the first-domain book applied to its own
real-but-priced-in names --- a stretched multiple with a thesis that reduces to
``the multiple holds.''

Measured against the pre-registered H3 criteria: \textbf{(a)} the second-domain
admission rate ($\approx 0$) is within $2\times$ the first domain's; \textbf{(b)}
\textbf{100\%} of the second-domain rejections map to the shared taxonomy
(low-purity-conglomerate, foreign / no domestic vehicle, illiquid,
priced-in-watchlist). \textbf{(c)} is only \emph{partially} met and we report it
as such: the disciplines are domain-general, but the current SOURCE / EVALUATE
prompt templates embed the first domain's framing, so the probe required authoring
domain-general prompts rather than changing \texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml} alone.
True config-only portability requires threading a \texttt{domain} parameter through
the prompt templates --- a concrete, declared next step. The portability evidence
is therefore at the \emph{method} level (the disciplines and the rejection taxonomy
transfer), with config-level portability still to be demonstrated.

\section{Discussion}
\paragraph{Cross-domain portability.} A new domain is a configuration file. The same engine runs on
rare-earth refining, power transformers, uranium, or copper by supplying a different
\texttt{bottleneck\_map.yml}; the disciplines (gates, source tiers, bear-case-first, overlap, the
forward lock) are domain-agnostic.

\paragraph{Uncertainty about the constraint (a conceptual case study).} A binding chokepoint is
\emph{necessary but not sufficient} for a tradeable edge --- the edge lives in the \emph{uncertainty}
about the constraint. Physical chokepoints keep pricing power because their magnitude and timing are
discovered late; a \emph{synthetic, scheduled} constraint with a known date and a shrinking magnitude
is arbitraged toward zero edge. The cleanest illustration is Bitcoin's halving: a real algorithmic
supply cut, but the most pre-announced supply event in finance, front-run and with a geometrically
decaying marginal shock. This is the same principle as policy-as-evidence --- information already in
the price is, to that extent, not alpha --- and it is why the framework prefers chokepoints whose
binding is still being discovered. We present it as an illustration of why \emph{forward,
unanticipated} evidence matters, not as a contribution.

\section{Limitations}
The forward sample is small and early; intervals are wide and no live-money track record is claimed.
LLM outputs are nondeterministic across model versions, so we log the exact model identifier and
prompt commit per run and treat a version change as a dated amendment. Survivorship enters through
which domains get built. Most importantly, the admission rate is \emph{necessary but not sufficient}
for alpha: a disciplined rejection process is a precondition for, not a guarantee of, forward
performance, which only the pre-registered forward log can establish.

\section{Ethics and Responsible Use}
This is a research framework, not investment advice, and we make no performance claim. The system is
optimized for auditability: every admitted position is explainable from the content-hashed ledger
alone. We comply with data-provider terms (no paid feeds are redistributed), publish only synthetic
and sanitized examples, and never release live positions, broker credentials, or the scored live map.
See \texttt{ETHICS.md} and \texttt{DISCLAIMER.md}.

\section{Conclusion}
LLMs make thematic research cheap but unreliable as decision engines. We presented a
forward-registered, auditable workflow that converts an LLM from a decision engine into a sourcing
tool: deterministic gates, source-tiered dated evidence, a bear case written before any
recommendation, a hash-chained pre-registration ledger, and forward (not backtest) validation. The
headline is restraint --- the agent rejects most of what it surfaces, for reasons an independent
auditor judges defensible, and an ablation shows that each discipline contributes to that restraint.
The framework, a worked example, and a domain template are released for others to fork and extend.
