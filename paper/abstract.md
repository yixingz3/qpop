# Abstract (draft)

Large language models make open-ended thematic investment research cheap, but unreliable: they
confabulate facts, chase narratives, over-admit ideas, and — when evaluated by backtest — overfit
periods that postdate their training data. We present **a pre-registered, agent-assisted research
framework** that wraps LLM-driven discovery in deterministic gates and a forward pre-registration
protocol, so that an idea can move capital only after surviving falsifiable, dated-evidence checks
and being validated *forward* on paper trading rather than by a fragile backtest.

The framework contributes: (1) a **domain-agnostic bottleneck graph** in which nodes are physical
chokepoints and a new domain is a configuration file, not new code; (2) a **decomposed confidence
model** (bottleneck × purity × demand × valuation × crowding) in which government policy enters as
*dated evidence and a reversal trigger, never as a multiplicative factor*; (3) a **budget-aware
funnel** — cheap-model sourcing, deterministic gating, expensive-model evaluation of finalists with
a bear case written before any recommendation; (4) **forward-QPOP**, a content-hashed, append-only
ledger that imports pre-registration discipline into agentic research; and (5) a **no-stories**
discipline stack (source tiers, fact-vs-announcement, overlap penalty, structural-vs-cyclical,
admission restraint).

Our central empirical finding is methodological rather than a return claim: a disciplined,
pre-registered agent **rejects most of the candidates it surfaces**, with a low and pre-committed
admission rate, while every admitted position remains explainable and auditable from the ledger
alone — in contrast to ungated LLM screeners, which over-admit by construction. We release the
framework, a worked AI-supply-chain example, and a template for new domains, and pre-register a
forward evaluation across one or more additional thematic domains.
