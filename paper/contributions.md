# Contributions

1. **A domain-agnostic bottleneck graph** for thematic research — nodes are physical chokepoints,
   edges are dependencies; a new domain (rare earth, power, gas, uranium, copper, defense
   industrial) is *a config file*, reusing the same engine.

2. **A decomposed confidence model** — `bottleneck × purity × demand × valuation × crowding` — that
   structurally prevents "right theme → buy the ticker," with **policy as dated evidence + a reversal
   trigger, never a multiplicative factor**.

3. **A budget-aware LLM funnel** — cheap-model sourcing → deterministic gating → expensive-model
   evaluation of finalists only, with a **bear case written before the recommendation**, and a
   tunable model-tier split (incl. a cheap-first-pass / expensive-adjudication mode for scarce
   pools).

4. **Forward-QPOP** — content-hashed, append-only pre-registration of every admission and belief
   update, validated **forward** on paper trading + conservative shadow fills, importing
   registered-report discipline into agentic investment research without a fragile backtest.

5. **A no-stories discipline stack** — source tiers; fact-vs-announcement; overlap penalty
   (replace, don't stack); structural-vs-cyclical; a position changes only on a fired pre-registered
   trigger, a binding cap, or a risk limit.

6. **An empirical finding about restraint** — a pre-registered, gated agent admits a small
   single-digit fraction of LLM-surfaced candidates, with each admission explainable and auditable;
   ablating any single discipline raises the admission rate, quantifying its contribution.

7. **An open, reproducible release** — the framework, a worked AI-supply-chain example, a
   `template_domain` for new domains, synthetic samples, and the methods paper — so others can fork,
   extend to new domains, and contribute forward results (the cross-domain flywheel).
