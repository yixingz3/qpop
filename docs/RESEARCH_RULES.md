# Research Rules — the no-stories contract

These rules are the discipline stack. They apply to every agent, every candidate, every confidence
change. They are domain-agnostic.

## Source tiers

| Tier | Examples | Can it establish confidence? |
|---|---|---|
| **primary** | filings, transcripts, official guidance, signed awards | yes |
| **secondary** | reputable trade press, market research | yes (with care) |
| **market-implied** | price, volume, options, short interest | yes (for crowding/valuation) |
| **tertiary** | social (X / Reddit / Substack), blogs | **no — idea seed only** |

A confidence move requires **≥ secondary or market-implied** corroboration. Tertiary sources may
*surface* an idea but can never *establish* it.

## No stories

> Every claim rests on a verifiable, **dated** fact. If you cannot verify it with a dated source,
> say so in `missing_info` — never assert a current fact from memory.

A position is reduced or exited **only** when a **fired pre-registered exit trigger**, a **binding
cap**, or a **risk limit** forces it. Never on a narrative, a vibe, or an after-the-fact rationale.
If a profit/loss move needs an ex-post story to explain it, the bet should not have been taken.

## Fact vs announcement

A **signed, dated, material** event (an executed award, a binding contract, a filed result) is
evidence. A **political pledge / headline / "intention"** is *watchlist-context only* — it requires
≥ secondary corroboration and a check on materiality before it can touch conviction. Funding
*certainty* (rumor → signed → disbursed), *materiality* (size vs the relevant base), and
*execution-link* (does it unlock real capacity?) are the questions; *durability / sentiment* are
forecasts and inform narrative, not a score.

## Structural vs cyclical

Distinguish a **hard chokepoint** (few suppliers, multi-year / multi-billion capacity, no
substitute) from a **commodity-cycle peak** riding a bullwhip. The cyclical name often *looks* most
like the winner (biggest recent move) and is the trap — its pricing power evaporates when lead
times compress ("the cure for high prices is high prices"). Rate many-supplier commodities lower.

## Bear case before recommendation

Every expensive-model evaluation **writes the case *against* admission first**, then recommends.
This defeats sycophancy and one-sided narratives. Most good-theme names should land *watchlist*; a
"no action" outcome is normal and correct.

## Overlap before novelty

Judge a candidate **against the book you already hold**, not in isolation. A great theme that
duplicates a held bet (high correlation, same node, same demand driver) must **replace, not
stack** — the gate is a *comparison*, not a veto. "Best name on the axis" is meaningless if the
axis itself is tiny or already owned.

## Auditability over cleverness

Every weight must be explainable by `score × purity × adjustments × caps × drift`. If any required
data is missing or stale, **skip the trade and explain why** — never force an action. Missing data
must use conservative fallbacks or block trading; it may never read as *zero* risk.
